Treasures of Anatolia

Turkey

6 May 2025 • Cappadocia Balloons

A Journey Through Turkey's Riches

From the ancient streets of Istanbul to the surreal landscapes of Cappadocia, the healing waters of Pamukkale, and the ruins of legendary Troy, this 10-day itinerary showcases the cultural and natural gems of Anatolia — the Asian heart of Turkey.

If you want to combine history, adventure, landscapes, and local charm, this route is the perfect introduction to the wonders of Turkey.

Day 1–2: Istanbul – Where East Meets West

Start your journey in Istanbul, the only city in the world that straddles two continents.

  • Eat your way through Turkish street food and rooftop mezze
Where to Stay: Sultanahmet for history or Karaköy for a trendy vibe

Day 3: Ankara – The Capital of Turkey

Travel to Ankara, the modern capital and a city often skipped by tourists — but worth a visit for its deep historical roots.

Highlights in Ankara:

  • Anıtkabir, the mausoleum of Atatürk
  • Museum of Anatolian Civilizations – perfect for history lovers
  • Stroll the old citadel (Kale) and enjoy panoramic city views
Book your highlights tour here
Travel Tip: High-speed trains or domestic flights make this route easy and efficient.

Day 4: Salt Lake (Tuz Gölü) & Arrival in Cappadocia

Drive toward Cappadocia, stopping en route at the dazzling Salt Lake (Tuz Gölü) — a surreal, mirror-like expanse that's especially stunning in spring or after rain.

Don't Miss:

  • Walking barefoot across the reflective salt flats
  • Photos at golden hour!

Arrive in Cappadocia by evening.

Day 5–6: Cappadocia – Land of Fairy Chimneys and Hot Air Balloons

Cappadocia feels like another planet — with its dreamlike rock formations, cave dwellings, and sunrise balloon rides.

Day 7: Pamukkale – Thermal Pools and Ancient Hierapolis

Travel to Pamukkale, famous for its white travertine terraces and thermal waters.

Things to Do:

  • Walk barefoot through the thermal pools of Pamukkale
  • Visit the ancient city of Hierapolis, with its amphitheatre and necropolis
  • Swim in Cleopatra's Pool, filled with ancient marble columns
    Book here

Day 8: Ephesus – A Step Back into Ancient Rome

Next, explore Ephesus, one of the best-preserved Roman cities in the world.

Highlights:

  • The grand Library of Celsus
  • Temple of Artemis (one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World)
  • Great Theatre and ancient marble streets
    Book here
  • Nearby House of Virgin Mary
Where to Stay: Selçuk or nearby coastal town Kusadasi for sea views

Day 9: Troy – Legends and Lost Cities

Head north to Troy, home of the legendary Trojan War and the famous wooden horse.

Things to Do:

  • Visit the ancient ruins of Troy, dating back over 4,000 years
  • See the reconstructed wooden horse and the archaeological layers of the city
  • Learn about Homer's Iliad and the history behind the legend
    Book here

Day 10: Return to Istanbul

Wrap up your Anatolian adventure with a scenic drive or flight back to Istanbul. Spend your final evening sipping Turkish tea or coffee with a Bosphorus view, shopping for souvenirs, or indulging in a traditional hammam (Turkish bath) Book here

Quick Travel Tips:

Best time to visit: April–June or September–October (mild weather, fewer crowds)
Transport: Mix of domestic flights, high-speed trains, and private transfers or tours
Language: Turkish is spoken, but English is common in tourist areas
Currency: Turkish Lira (₺)
Packing tip: Bring modest clothing for mosques, comfy shoes for ruins, and layers for changing climates

Final Thoughts

This 10-day journey through Anatolia is a whirlwind of ancient wonders, natural beauty, and Turkish hospitality. From the streets of Istanbul to the skies of Cappadocia, and the ruins of Troy to the healing waters of Pamukkale — this route will leave you awe-inspired and wanting more.
