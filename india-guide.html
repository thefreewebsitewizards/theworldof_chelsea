The Ultimate Golden Triangle + Ranthambore Itinerary

India is an explosion of culture, color, chaos, and calm — all in one unforgettable journey. If you're planning your first trip, this 10-day itinerary is the perfect introduction, covering the iconic Golden Triangle: Delhi, Jaipur, and Agra, with a wild detour to Ranthambore National Park for a chance to spot Bengal tigers.

This itinerary blends palaces, forts, bustling bazaars, spiritual sites, and serene landscapes — giving you a taste of India's incredible diversity.

Day 1–2: Discover Delhi – The Capital of Contrasts

Start your journey in Delhi, where ancient history meets modern chaos. It's loud, colorful, and completely captivating.

Top Things to Do in Delhi:

  • Marvel at Humayun's Tomb, the Mughal masterpiece that inspired the Taj Mahal
  • Take a rickshaw ride through Old Delhi's Chandni Chowk and visit Jama Masjid
    Book here
  • Admire India Gate, the Presidential Palace, and the broad avenues of Lutyens' Delhi
  • Explore Qutub Minar, a UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Visit Lotus Temple or Akshardham for a spiritual experience
Where to Stay: Connaught Place (central) or Aerocity (near airport)
Tip: Hire a local guide for Old Delhi — navigating the lanes, spice markets, and food stalls is much easier with expert help.

Day 3–4: Jaipur – The Pink City of Palaces

Travel by car or train to Jaipur, Rajasthan's capital, famous for its architecture, pink-hued buildings, and royal heritage.

Top Things to Do in Jaipur:

  • Explore Amber Fort, with sweeping views and ornate interiors
  • Stop at Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds) for iconic Instagram shots
  • Tour the City Palace and Jantar Mantar, the royal observatory
    Book your tour here
  • Wander Patrika Gate and the vibrant Bapu Bazaar
  • Optional: Experience a traditional Rajasthani dinner at Chokhi Dhani
Where to Stay: A restored haveli (heritage mansion) for a truly authentic stay

Day 5: Safari Adventure in Ranthambore National Park

Leave early for Ranthambore (approx. 3–4 hours from Jaipur) — one of India's most famous national parks and your chance to see wild tigers in their natural habitat.

What to Do:

  • Afternoon jeep safari through the park's rugged terrain
    Book here
  • Visit the ancient Ranthambore Fort for epic views and a taste of history
Where to Stay: Jungle lodges or boutique resorts like The Tigress, Sher Bagh, or Nahargarh

Day 6: Sunrise Safari & Travel to Agra

Start your day with one more safari (your best chance to see a tiger!) before driving to Agra (6–7 hours). Arrive in time to catch a view of the Taj Mahal from across the river at Mehtab Bagh or relax with rooftop dining.

Day 7: Agra – Home of the Taj Mahal

No trip to India is complete without visiting its most iconic landmark.

Top Things to Do in Agra:

  • Watch the sunrise at the Taj Mahal — go early to beat the crowds
    Book a combined ticket here
  • Explore Agra Fort, once the main residence of the Mughal emperors
  • Visit Itimad-ud-Daulah, often called the "Baby Taj"
  • Stroll the gardens of Mehtab Bagh for postcard-worthy views of the Taj
Tip: Sunrise is the best time for photos and fewer people — don't skip it!

Day 8–9: Return to Delhi + Optional Day Trip

Drive back to Delhi (3–4 hours) and enjoy a slower pace to wrap up your trip.

Ideas for a Relaxed Day 9:

  • Unwind in Lodhi Gardens or explore Hauz Khas Village
  • Visit the stunning Akshardham Temple
  • Shop for last-minute souvenirs at Dilli Haat or Khan Market
  • Treat yourself to a spa afternoon or rooftop dinner

Day 10: Departure

Spend your final morning soaking up the last moments of India — grab street food, sip chai, and reflect on an adventure of a lifetime before heading to the airport.

Travel Essentials

Best time to go: October to March (cooler, dry season)
Visas: Most nationalities need an e-visa — apply online before arrival
Transport: Book trains in advance or hire a private car with a driver for smoother travel
Packing tips: Modest clothing, light layers, hand sanitiser, and a power adapter
Connectivity: Local SIM or eSIM helps with maps, bookings, and communication

Final Thoughts

India is intense, beautiful, and humbling — and this 10-day itinerary gives you just the right taste of its magic. From the grandeur of the Taj to the thrill of tiger spotting, this journey blends culture, wildlife, and wonder.
