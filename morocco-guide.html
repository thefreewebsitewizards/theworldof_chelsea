Morocco

Marrakech to the Sahara & Beyond

10 Days of Magic in North Africa

An Unforgettable Journey Through North Africa

Exotic souks, dramatic desert landscapes, ancient cities, and coastal charm — Morocco is an unforgettable journey through North Africa's most enchanting sights. In just 10 days, you can experience the magic of Marrakech, the towering Atlas Mountains, a night in the Sahara Desert, the cultural heart of Fes, and the coastal beauty of Essaouira.

This well-balanced itinerary takes you deep into the heart of Morocco's culture, architecture, and adventure.

Day 1–2: Marrakech – The Red City

Start your journey in vibrant Marrakech, a city full of energy, history, and sensory overload.

Top Things to Do in Marrakech:

  • Wander the Medina and explore the souks
  • Visit the stunning Bahia Palace and El Badi Palace
    Combine both and book here
  • Relax in the Jardin Majorelle and Yves Saint Laurent Museum
    Book here
  • Experience the chaos of Jemaa el-Fnaa Square at night
  • Stay in a riad (traditional Moroccan guesthouse) for the full experience - I would recommend Riad Goloboy
Tip: Don't miss the rooftop restaurants at sunset! - My favourite Kabana Bar

Day 3: Atlas Mountains & Aït Benhaddou

Leave the city behind and head for the scenic Atlas Mountains. En route, visit Tizi n'Tichka Pass, the highest road in Morocco, with epic views.

Highlights:

  • Traditional Berber villages nestled in the mountains
  • Visit the ancient ksar of Aït Benhaddou, a UNESCO World Heritage Site used in films like Gladiator and Game of Thrones
  • Optional: Do a day trip instead from Marrakech
    Book here
Overnight: Stay in or near Aït Benhaddou or Ouarzazate

Day 4–5: The Sahara Desert – Merzouga & Erg Chebbi

No trip to Morocco is complete without a night in the Sahara Desert.

Experience Includes:

  • A camel trek into the golden dunes of Erg Chebbi
  • Watching the sunset over the Sahara
  • Sleeping under the stars in a luxury desert camp
  • Traditional Berber music and dinner by the fire
Book here
Optional Stop: Todra Gorge or Dades Valley on the way to Merzouga for dramatic canyons
Overnight: I'd recommend staying at Dwo Golden Camp

Day 6–7: Fes – Morocco's Cultural Capital

Travel north to Fes, one of the oldest and most authentic medinas in the world.

Top Things to Do in Fes:

  • Get lost in the UNESCO-listed Fes el-Bali medina
  • Visit the Al Quaraouiyine University, the world's oldest
  • Explore the colorful Chouara Tannery
  • See Bou Inania Madrasa and Royal Palace gates
  • Shop for ceramics and traditional crafts
Tip: Hire a guide — Fes medina is easy to get lost in! Book here
Where to Stay: Riad Reda

Day 8: Essaouira – The Windy City by the Sea

Make your way west to the relaxed, artistic coastal town of Essaouira — a complete change of pace.

Highlights:

  • Wander the blue-and-white medina and ramparts
  • Stroll along the beachfront or try surfing/kitesurfing
  • Eat fresh seafood at the port grill stalls
  • Visit artisan workshops for woodcrafts and argan oil
Where to Stay: La Perle Mergador or head back to Marrakech and book a day trip from there Day trip option

Wrap up your adventure back where you started — with time to unwind, do last-minute shopping, or indulge in a traditional hammam spa.

Travel Tips for Morocco

Best time to visit: March–May or September–November for mild weather
Currency: Moroccan Dirham (MAD) some places accept Euros
Dress code: Dress modestly, especially in rural areas
Languages: Arabic, Berber, French — English is widely spoken in tourist areas
Getting around: Use a driver/guide or join a small group tour for smoother travel between cities

Final Thoughts

This 10-day Morocco itinerary captures the essence of the country — vibrant souks, cinematic landscapes, ancient cities, and unforgettable desert nights. Whether you're sipping mint tea in a riad or riding camels across golden dunes, Morocco will leave you spellbound.
